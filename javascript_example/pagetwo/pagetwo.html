II

Who am I to touch wet grief, to hunger
for root without accepting transformation?

I expect acidic sand to chew through
rattled and moth-eaten nostalgia. Instead

I sound ancestral name and find that behind
glass sympathies, death may weave tiny wonder

or, at least, hold time’s quiet shriek. Mouth filled
with poor stone, I bend the ocean’s truth, rename

euphoric church called sky for iridescent
depths. These innocent breaks slip

golden cartilage into hard shapes, burn lost twilight
to blood’s fog. Inside longing I fill the chest plate,

squeeze a pillar of salt from the moon’s shell
into figures who forget

their opportunity to transform anxious grief
to fatted calf and absent mirror.


iii

Who am I to mourn dark soil, hunger
for root without accepting transformation?
I expect sand to chew acidic through

purpled visions of thatched roof. Instead
I sound ancestral name and find that within
trilling notes new maps weave tiny wonder,

at least hold quiet shriek. Mouth filled with
old pebbles, I bend ocean slumber, rename
euphoric temple called sky for iridescent

depths. These innocent breaks slip golden
cartilage into hard shapes, burn twilight
to blood’s fog. Inside longing I fill the chest plate,

squeeze pillars of salt from moon shells,
form figures who do not realize
in cosmic fear the fatted calf drips gore.
